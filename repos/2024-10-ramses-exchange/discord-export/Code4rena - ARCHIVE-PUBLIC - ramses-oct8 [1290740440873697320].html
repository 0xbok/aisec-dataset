DOG | Ramses 10/09/2024 16:42
Hey everyone, dropping in to give some answers to some FAQs to help provide more clarity: Q: What are some differences between UniV3 and RamsesV3? A1: UniswapV3 is =0.7.6, with no production-ready ^0.8 compatible release. We had to adapt all the math and accounting that relies on under/overflows to have proper unchecked blocks and flip signs where needed. A2: Ramses has a ve-governance system, also known as a MetaDEX or "Solidly" model. As such, we reward liquidity providers via our GaugeV3s, based on accounting in the libraries, which communicate with the V3Pool to allow a non-custodial CL rewarding system. Our Gauges are the first hardened A3: The aforementioned rewarding system relies mainly on the following contracts-- (GaugeV3, Position, Tick, Oracle, RamsesV3Pool). The reward accounting uses a debt system which is updated upon necessary interactions. A4: Users can claim gauge rewards (emissions or any whitelisted LP incentives), on a per-second basis -- accruing in real-time while in range. A5: feeProtocol moved away from the bitshifting and does a linear (0-100) system for feeSplits. e.g. 10 feeProtocol = 10% of swap fees go to the FeeCollector, and 90% goes to liquidity providers. This is normally 80% to the FeeCollector by default. A6: In UniswapV3, pools are mapped by their feeTiers to tickSpacing. In RamsesV3 we map pools by their tickspacing explicitly, mitigating duplicate tickspacing pools with different initial fee tiers A7: UniswapV3 pools do not have dynamic (Adjustable) swap fees while Ramses' do. Q: UniswapV3 is already battle-tested / hardened, why does it need to be audited redundantly? A: While UniswapV3 is infact very secure, we rewrote for ^0.8 and compile in viaIr; which has a lot of nuances regarding the math/accounting. Q: What is a period? Why does this matter? A: A period is a colloquial way to refer to an epoch. Periods align with the timestamps of the start of the epoch. [0 UTC Thursday -> 23:59 UTC Wednesday] A2: GaugeV3 rewards are distributed per period, and can be retroactive/future-earned depending on the period notified and current time within the epoch. Happy to answer any other questions as they come as well happy hunting. (edited)
